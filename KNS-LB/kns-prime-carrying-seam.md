## KREIN-RANK1: the KSSF face of the same wall

For rank-one moves A → A + λ(·,φ)φ, the Krein spectral shift u is exactly the
**phase shift of the spectral-measure pair** (μ,ν):
`1 + πλ𝒦μ = exp[𝒦u] = [1 − πλ𝒦ν]^{−1}` ([P96] §3, Lemma 3.1). Constraints:

- **[P96] Theorem 4.1.** All self-adjoint rank-one perturbations of A are
  pure point ⟺ σ(A) is countable. Corollary 4.4 extends to trace class.
- **Weyl–von Neumann stability.** Essential spectrum is invariant under
  rank-one (and trace-class) moves — invoked in [P96]'s own proof of 2⇒1.

**Consequence.** On the K_σ / n⁴-ladder side, no bounded, trace-class, or
KSSF-mediated rank-one injection changes the counting class. The spectral-
shift function is pair data; perturbing a fixed geometric operator can only
redistribute what its essential spectrum already permits. Register as wall
face `KREIN-RANK1` ⊂ Weyl-stability family (unification with existing
walls; no new obstruction, one new face).
